💡 Best Practices & Prompt Engineering

Master Effective Prompting

Learn the fundamentals of writing clear, effective prompts that generate high-quality AI outputs.

The CRAFT Framework

Every effective prompt contains five key elements. Use CRAFT to structure your prompts:

Element Purpose Example
C — Context Background information about your situation "I'm planning an AP audit at a healthcare organization with 150 vendors."
R — Role The expertise the AI should adopt "Act as an experienced internal auditor."
A — Action The specific task you need "Identify 8-10 key risks in this process."
F — Format How you want the output structured "Numbered list with title, description, and impact rating."
T — Tone The style appropriate for your audience "Professional, suitable for audit committee."

5 Best Ways to Write Prompts

1. Be Specific, Not General

❌ Weak: "Write a procedure."

✅ Better: "Write a step-by-step procedure for validating vendor master data changes, including who performs each step and what system they use."

2. Provide Clear Context

❌ Weak: "Is this a good control?"

✅ Better: "This control requires monthly reconciliations by custodians reviewed by supervisors. Is this adequate to prevent unauthorized fund transfers?"

3. Give Constraints

❌ Weak: "Generate interview questions."

✅ Better: "Generate 6-8 open-ended interview questions (each under 2 sentences) for the AP manager about the three-way matching process."

4. Show Examples

❌ Weak: "Write a finding."

✅ Better: "Write a finding similar to this one: [good example]. Now apply the same structure to: [your situation]."

5. Iterate & Refine

First prompt rarely perfect. Follow up with: "Good start. Add more specificity to the impact section" or "Strengthen the recommendation."

Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Vague Instructions ▼

Problem: The AI doesn't know what you really want.

Fix: Be specific. Instead of "analyze this," try "analyze this control for design adequacy and list three potential failure points."

❌ Missing Context ▼

Problem: The AI can't tailor output to your organization.

Fix: Provide org type, size, industry, and situation. "This is a public university with 500 cardholders..."

❌ Too Many Tasks ▼

Problem: Complex outputs lose quality and focus.

Fix: Break into separate prompts. First draft the finding, then recommend actions, then justify ratings separately.

❌ Assuming AI Memory ▼

Problem: "Update this as we discussed" — the AI has no memory of prior conversations.

Fix: Be explicit. "Modify by adding: (1) quantification, (2) clarity on root cause, (3) stronger recommendation."

❌ Ignoring AI Limitations ▼

Problem: Trusting AI output without verification.

Fix: Always verify facts, policy citations, and calculations. AI can "hallucinate" (confidently state false information).

Quality Review Before Using AI Output

Always verify these items before using AI output:

  • ✅ Accuracy: Are all facts, policy references, and citations correct?
  • ✅ Completeness: Does it address all aspects of your request?
  • ✅ Relevance: Is it specific to your organization and situation?
  • ✅ Objectivity: Is the language neutral and professional?
  • ✅ Clarity: Will your audience understand this?
  • ✅ Alignment: Does it match other documentation?

Prompts vs. AI Agents: Key Differences

Understanding when to use each:

Aspect Prompt AI Agent
What It Does You give instructions, AI responds once AI system works autonomously on multi-step tasks
Complexity Single or linear tasks Complex, adaptive, decision-making workflows
System Access Only text generation Can integrate with APIs, databases, email, etc.
Autonomy Requires human prompt for each task Works independently once configured
Use Case: Example "Generate a risk assessment." "Daily: Extract AP transactions, flag exceptions, email report."
Time to Execute Seconds to minutes Minutes to hours
Setup Difficulty Easy — write text instructions Moderate to complex — requires configuration

When to Use Prompts: Drafting, analyzing documents, brainstorming, generating questions, explaining concepts.

When to Use Agents: Recurring automated tasks, continuous monitoring, data quality checks, workflow automation.

Your Responsibility as an Auditor

AI augments — does not replace — professional judgment.

You remain responsible for:

  • Verifying all AI output for accuracy
  • Applying professional skepticism
  • Ensuring organization-specific accuracy
  • Maintaining audit independence
  • Documenting use of AI per your organization's policy
