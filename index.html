1 Introduction to AI in Internal Audit

1.1 Purpose of This Manual

This training manual provides internal auditors with comprehensive guidance on using Large Language Models (LLMs) to enhance audit efficiency and effectiveness. It accompanies the Internal Audit AI Prompt Library — a structured collection of pre-built prompts aligned with IIA Global Internal Audit Standards (2025).

By the end, you'll master prompt engineering and confidently use the library while maintaining professional standards.

1.2 The Evolution of AI in Auditing

1980s: Spreadsheets introduced
1990s: Computer-Assisted Audit Techniques (CAATs)
2010s: Data analytics become mainstream
2020s: Generative AI & LLMs — the next transformational shift

LLMs understand natural language, analyze unstructured text, generate content, and assist with complex reasoning — invaluable for judgment-intensive audit work.

2 Understanding Large Language Models

2.1 What Is a Large Language Model?

An LLM is an AI system trained on vast text data to understand and generate human-like language. Think of it as an extremely well-read assistant that has studied billions of documents. It generates responses based on patterns — it does not search the internet in real-time (unless enabled) or access your systems.

Probabilistic OutputTap to reveal
Responses are generated word-by-word based on probability. Same prompt may yield slightly different outputs. Always review.
Context WindowTap to reveal
Limited memory per conversation (measured in tokens). Very long documents may need to be processed in sections.
Knowledge CutoffTap to reveal
Training data has an end date; no real-time info. Verify current policies, regulations & org changes.
No True UnderstandingTap to reveal
Pattern matching, not genuine comprehension. May produce plausible-sounding but incorrect information.

2.2 Capabilities & Limitations

✅ What LLMs Do Well: Drafting/editing, summarizing, brainstorming risks/controls, explaining concepts, structuring info, generating interview questions, comparing documents.

❌ What LLMs Cannot Do: Access your systems, guaranteed-accurate math, replace professional judgment, guarantee factual accuracy (may hallucinate), understand unprovided context, exercise professional skepticism.

⚠️ CRITICAL — Hallucinations: LLMs can generate confident-sounding but completely false information. Always verify factual claims, especially policy/regulation citations.

2.4 Security & Confidentiality

DATA PROTECTION REQUIREMENTS: Before using AI with audit info, understand your org's policies on: data entry, enterprise vs. consumer versions, data retention, and handling of PII. When in doubt, anonymize.

Best practices: Remove names/IDs, use enterprise versions, never enter passwords/SSNs, consider legal discoverability, document AI use per policy.

3 Prompt Engineering Fundamentals

3.1 What Is Prompt Engineering?

The skill of crafting effective instructions to get useful AI outputs. Think of it as giving instructions to a highly capable but literal-minded assistant. The more specific and clear, the better the results.

3.4 Prompting Techniques

Zero-Shot Prompting ▼

Ask directly without examples. Works for straightforward tasks.
Example: "List five common fraud risks in accounts payable."

Few-Shot Prompting ▼

Provide examples of desired output to guide format and quality.
Example: "Write a finding title following these examples: 'Incomplete Vendor Due Diligence...' Now write one for: 15% of POs approved after goods receipt."

Chain-of-Thought Prompting ▼

Ask AI to work step-by-step, showing reasoning. Great for complex analysis.
Example: "Analyze this control. Step 1: Identify the risk. Step 2: Assess design adequacy. Step 3: Identify failure points. Step 4: Conclude."

Iterative Refinement ▼

Start basic, review output, refine with follow-ups. Often the most effective approach.
Initial → "Good start. Add quantification." → "Strengthen recommendation."

6 Quality Assurance & Professional Judgment

6.1 The Human-AI Partnership

AI augments — does not replace — professional judgment. The internal auditor remains responsible for all work product quality. Think of AI as a capable research assistant that needs your expert review.

Auditor Responsibilities: Verify facts, ensure org-specific accuracy, apply professional skepticism, maintain independence, document AI use.

6.2 Quality Review Checklist

Tap each item to check it off:

✓ Accuracy: All facts, policy references & standards citations are correct
✓ Completeness: Output addresses all aspects of the request
✓ Relevance: Content is specific to my organization & engagement
✓ Objectivity: Language is neutral and free from bias
✓ Clarity: Intended audience will understand this
✓ Consistency: Aligns with other engagement documentation

6.4 Avoiding Over-Reliance on AI

New auditors should: understand methodology before using prompts, attempt tasks manually occasionally, use methodology notes as learning opportunities, and discuss AI outputs with supervisors to calibrate judgment.

7 Advanced Prompting Techniques

7.1 Prompt Chaining

Break complex tasks into sequential prompts where each output feeds the next:

Step 1: Analyze → Step 2: Quantify → Step 3: Research → Step 4: Draft

7.2 Using AI for Data Analysis

LLMs can suggest anomalies to look for, write SQL/Excel queries, interpret results, and recommend visualizations.

⚠️ Never paste sensitive raw data unless permitted. Use anonymized samples or summary statistics.

7.3 Customizing Library Prompts

Add organization-specific context (terminology, policy references), incorporate industry knowledge (FERPA, Title IV, NCAA for higher ed), and adjust complexity based on engagement scope.

Glossary of Key Terms

Chain-of-Thought Prompting ▼

Technique where AI shows step-by-step reasoning, improving accuracy for complex tasks.

Context Window ▼

The amount of text an LLM can process at once, measured in tokens.

Hallucination ▼

When AI generates confident-sounding but factually incorrect information, including fake citations.

Prompt Engineering ▼

The skill of crafting effective prompts to achieve desired AI outputs.

Token ▼

Basic unit of text processing for LLMs; roughly equivalent to a word or word fragment.
